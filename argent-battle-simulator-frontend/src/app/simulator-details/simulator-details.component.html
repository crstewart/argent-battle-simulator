The Argent Battle Simulator takes a one-on-one duel between two characters and plays out the battle between them.

Overview

The Argent Battle Simulator is written in Java. The source code is also available on GitHub (link in the footer) if you want to take a peek at the inner workings of the simulator.

The Argent Battle Simulator runs battles on a 5x1 grid in a turn-based RPG manner. The fighters start at positions 2 and 4 on the grid, facing each other. Every turn both fighters take one action; they have the option to attack, defend, or move. The decision is made using a strategy based on weighted RNG. The weights for various actions are set at the beginning of the battle. While most fighters are weighted in a balanced manner between attack and defense, some are weighted toward attacks while others favor defensive tactics. Weights also change over time as the fighter determines how effective each action is against the opponent.

Attacks are split into three categories: Melee, ranged, and magic. Damage done by these attacks is determined by a mathematical formula based on the power and variance assigned to the attack plus the stats for a character. The speed of the attack is determined by the attack’s speed. If a character is struck by an attack before they can execute their own action, they have a chance of being stunned by the faster attack. When stunned a character cannot act for that turn. Attacks that require melee range have their speed in part determined by how far away they are from the other character.

Fighters each have a set of statistics to help decide how they fight. Most of these stats will be familiar to those who play RPGs:

  • HP is the amount of damage a character can take before they are KO’d.
  • SP is the amount of magic or special power that is used or built up over time for certain attacks. This only applies to certain characters.
  • Strength increases a fighter’s damage with melee attacks. It also factors into the ability not to be stunned by opposing melee attacks.
  • Toughness determines the fighter’s ability to resist stun from melee and ranged attacks.
  • Dexterity factors into the ability to hit with melee attacks. It also increases damage done with ranged attacks.
  • Aim determines a character’s ability to hit with ranged attacks, as well as their critical hit chance.
  • Agility factors into a character’s ability to critical hit with melee and ranged attacks. Higher numbers also allow them to dodge better.
  • Speed factors into a character’s dodge chance. It also allows them to move on the battlefield faster.
  • Intellect increases a fighter’s damage with magic attacks. It also affects the amount of weight given to a particularly effective or ineffective action when determining strategy for future turns. (Higher intellect means they will use effective attacks more often.)
  • Willpower helps determine the critical hit chance of magic attacks. It also affects the stun chance of incoming magic attacks.
  • Size factors into the chance an incoming attack will miss the fighter. Smaller characters are harder to hit.
  • Weight does not take much into account except for certain attacks that use this number.

X-Strike

Every character in the simulator has a special attack known as the X-Strike. This attack is their most powerful move. X-Strikes require charging through various actions over the course of a battle. The X-Strike meter fills up infinitely during a battle; however, once a character hits 20 on their meter they can spend 20 X-Strike points to use this powerful attack. X-Strikes cannot be blocked or dodged. X-Strikes have a high weight in all strategies, so it is likely they are used once their meter fills enough.

X-Strike meter is filled up by characters using the following rules:

  • Every character passively gains 1 point on their X-Strike meter each turn.
  • A successful attack adds an additional 1 X-Strike point to their meter.
  • A successful dodge adds 2 extra X-Strike points to their meter.
  • A successful block adds 3 additional X-Strikes points to their meter.

All-in-all, a successful guard adds 4 points to a character’s X-Strike meter in a single turn! Defensive characters gain X-Strike points faster than others, so it is entirely likely that they use their X-Strike faster than anyone else.

Defenses

Some characters have specific strengths and weaknesses within the simulator. There are a lot of different attack types within the simulator. For example, Slash attacks come from swords and daggers while Explosive attacks come from bombs and grenades. All attacks, including X-Strikes, have their own attack type or types. The effectiveness of an attack (and whether or not it is weighted higher or lower by a character mid-fight) is based on how strong or weak the opponent is to that attack.

Traits

Some characters in the simulator have special traits that aid them passively in battle. The list of traits are as follows:

  • Rival allows a hero to deal extra damage to the villain they fight most often. For example, Mario deals 10% additional damage to Bowser on all his attacks.
  • Natural Agility allows a character to dodge gunfire, marked as BULLET_SPEED in the character descriptions.
  • Hazardous applies to characters with spiky bodies. They deal small amounts of passive damage to anyone who attacks them in physical combat (punches, kicks, headbutts, etc.)
  • Mech Rider applies to characters like Dr. Wily and Tron Bonne who ride mechs into battle. They have a passive damage reduction on all incoming attacks. However, critical hits apply additional damage to them.
  • Stealth Detection negates the dodge bonus applied to stealth attacks.
  • Bounty Hunter allows a character to deal a small amount of additional damage to humanoid and alien characters.
  • Maverick Hunter is unique to Zero. It allows him to deal additional damage to mechs and mech riders.
  • Ex-Maverick Hunter is unique to Sigma. It allows him to deal a small amount of bonus damage to humanoids and mechs.
  • Monster Hunter allows a character to deal additional damage to monster characters like Mewtwo and Sans.
  • Animal Hunter allows a character to deal additional damage to animal characters like Sonic and Pikachu.
  • Dragon Slayer allows a character to deal a large amount of additional damage to dragon characters such as Charizard and Spyro.
  • Demon Slayer allows a character to deal additional damage to characters with demonic elements such as Akuma and Diablo.
  • Barrier Change is unique to Magus. Whenever he uses a spell, he gains a large weakness to the same element as the attack. However, he also gains passive armor against all attacks of the elements at his disposal that he does not use.
  • Trickster is unique to Joker. Whenever he summons a persona, he gains the weaknesses and resistances of the summon.

Battle Logs

Every battle in the simulator creates a battle log that is made available via download link. It gives a full description of how the battle went for the fighters in the simulator. A typical log looks like this:

Today's battle: Mario vs. Sonic!
Mario: 'Here we go!'
Sonic: 'Sonic's the name. Speed's my game.'
FIGHT!
----- Turn 1 -----
Sonic readies their Wind-Up Punch attack!
Mario attempts to dodge!
Mario dodges the attack!
Status: Mario (150/150) [3/20] vs Sonic (140/140) [1/20]
----- Turn 2 -----
Sonic retreats!
Mario attacks Sonic with Koopa Toss!
Sonic takes 20 damage!
Sonic (120/140) [2/20]
Status: Mario (150/150) [5/20] vs Sonic (120/140) [2/20]
...
----- Turn 15 -----
Mario unleashes their X-STRIKE!
Mario attacks Sonic with Mario Finale!
Sonic takes 54 damage!
Sonic (0/140) [19/20]
Status: Mario (30/150) [14/20] vs Sonic (0/140) [19/20]
Mario wins!
Mario: 'Yahoo! Mario's the winner!

HP is shown in parentheses () and X-Strike meter is shown in brackets []. Each character enters with one of their entry lines chosen at random. When a character wins, they celebrate with a victory quote.
